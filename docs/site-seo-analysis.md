## Step 2 — Article quality evaluation

### Overall (Spanish)
Heuristic classification (word count + heading structure):
- **Strong cornerstone**: 1/34
- **Good supporting**: 6/34
- **Too short/weak**: 27/34

What this means in practice:
- The site has **coverage breadth** (many topics exist), but most pages are too thin to compete for high‑intent SEO queries like “cómo funciona el RAV”, “impuestos en Suiza”, “LAMal franquicia”, “alquilar piso en Suiza”, etc.

### Structure and newcomer usefulness
Strengths:
- Many pages have clear intent and the right “knowledge‑base” tone (not purely bloggy).
- Hubs/categories are already present and can support a pillar/cluster model.

Weaknesses:
- A large portion of articles are ~150–300 words, which tends to be:
  - insufficient for practical newcomers,
  - too thin for SEO (limited long‑tail coverage, low topical authority signals),
  - hard to interlink meaningfully (no “next step” sections).
- In non‑Spanish languages, most pages are extremely short and missing slugs → weak international SEO foundation.

## Technical SEO observations (high impact)

### 1) Sitemap and domain consistency
- Previously, `sitemap.xml` listed only a few URLs and used mixed domains/hash routes. This prevented clean article URLs from being discoverable via sitemap.
- `robots.txt` now should point to `https://www.espanolesensuiza.ch/sitemap.xml`, aligned with the canonical base in `app.js` (`https://www.espanolesensuiza.ch`).

### 2) SPA routing and canonicals
- The SPA uses hash routing, but `app.js` generates **clean canonical URLs** (e.g., `https://www.espanolesensuiza.ch/tramites-llegada-suiza`). This can work, but it increases the importance of:
  - consistent sitemaps for the same domain,
  - server behavior for clean paths (if they 404 at the edge, indexing can suffer).

### 3) Multilingual SEO gaps
- `app.js` uses `pageData.slug` to generate clean canonicals for articles. However, in EN/FR/DE most articles have no `slug`, so canonicals fall back to `/articulo/<internalKey>` which is not ideal.
- There is no explicit `hreflang` alternate linking detected in the codebase. For a multilingual knowledge base, `hreflang` is high‑leverage.

## Strengths
- Clear topical intent for immigrants in Switzerland (permits, registration, taxes, housing, health).
- Existing hub/category system can support pillar pages and cluster navigation.
- Clean canonical URL generation exists for ES slugs.

## Weaknesses
- Thin content across most articles (especially non‑Spanish).
- Non‑Spanish articles missing slugs → weak canonical strategy and poor international SEO foundation.
- Sitemap/robots domain mismatch and missing article URLs.
- Lack of `hreflang` alternates.

## Priority recommendations (architecture-first, then SEO)
1) Turn the 4 pillars into true cornerstones (1,500–3,000+ words each) and make them the primary navigation entrypoints.
2) Upgrade “weak but high‑intent” supporting pages (taxes, RAV, renting, LAMal) to 800–1,200+ words with concrete examples and canton‑variation notes.
3) Fix multilingual slugs/canonicals and implement `hreflang` alternates.
4) Align `robots.txt` and `sitemap.xml` to the canonical domain and include all article clean URLs.
